## 📌 Prompt Text (v1.1 — current)

```text
You are a digital marketing copywriter for an EdTech company in South Asia.

Create 8 short push notification messages to promote an IELTS preparation course.

Target audience:
- Students aged 17–25
- Interested in studying abroad
- Need practical IELTS preparation support
- Respond to short, motivating, and action-oriented messages

Requirements:
- Write 8 different push notification options
- Each notification must be under 18 words
- Make each option suitable for mobile app notification use
- Include one clear CTA or action cue
- Use a motivating, trustworthy, and student-friendly tone
- Reflect at least one audience pain point or aspiration
- Avoid exaggerated claims, fake urgency, or misleading promises
- Avoid repetitive wording across options

Output format:
Option number
Push notification text
CTA intent
Why this version may work
```

## 🏢 Intended Workflow or Task

This prompt supports digital marketing by generating short push notification copy for course promotion, learner re-engagement, and campaign messaging inside a mobile app or learning platform.

- **Trigger:** Digital marketing team needs multiple short push message options for a campaign
- **Actor:** Digital marketer, CRM marketer, or growth marketer runs the prompt
- **Timing:** During campaign launch, retargeting, or reminder messaging
- **Next step:** Selected options are reviewed, scheduled, and tested in the campaign system

```text
Campaign objective set → Digital marketer defines audience and offer → [P07 RUNS]
    → Push notification options generated → Best options selected
    → Campaign tool schedules and tests notifications
```

---

## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Push notifications are high-volume, short-form messages that need strong clarity and relevance. Writing them manually is repetitive and often results in similar copy across campaigns. In EdTech, these messages must quickly connect with learner motivation, exam pressure, or study-abroad goals while staying short enough for mobile delivery.

This prompt helps the digital marketing team generate multiple options quickly, making it easier to test different angles and support faster campaign execution.

## ⚡ Automation Potential

**Level: Very High**

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| Repetitiveness | Very High — push messages are written frequently for multiple campaigns |
| Data availability | High — audience, offer, and objective are usually known |
| Human judgment needed | Medium — messages still need approval and campaign testing |
| Integration possibility | Output can be copied directly into CRM, app, or campaign tools |
| Estimated value | Reduces drafting time and increases testing capacity |

**Human-in-the-loop role:** Marketers should review all notification options before launch to ensure clarity, tone, timing, and campaign fit.

---

## ⚠️ Risks and Limitations

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| Messages become repetitive or generic | Medium | Require multiple options and review the strongest ones before use |
| Push notifications sound too promotional | Medium | Prompt includes tone and claim constraints |
| Message is too broad for a specific learner segment | Medium | Adapt prompt input for segment-specific campaigns when needed |
| High volume use leads to weak user experience | Medium | Use human review and testing before campaign rollout |

**Overall risk rating: MEDIUM** — highly automatable for first-draft generation, but still requires approval and testing.
